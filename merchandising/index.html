Q&A

Common questions before sending the merchandising quote request.

The page is designed to help you shortlist properly first. These are the points that most often need clarifying before the quote stage.

How does pricing work on this page?

Pricing is indicative and updates according to the option combination selected. It helps frame the likely price level before we review the final scope in detail.

Are GSM options available on every product?

Not always. Some products may only have one GSM option available in the current data, while others can support broader option sets later as the CSV evolves.

Can colours affect the price?

Yes. The pricing layer is prepared to account for colour-related adjustments where relevant, even if some products currently use only a lighter indicative structure.

Which branding methods can be selected?

At minimum the catalog supports embroidery, screen printing, DTF, and other methods present in the data. The exact methods offered depend on the product.

Can we combine several product families in one request?

Yes. The recap is built to collect different product types in one quote brief, as long as the final request stays commercially coherent.

Do you offer samples?

Sampling can be reviewed once the product shortlist and branding route are clearer. The most sensible route depends on the product family, the quantity, and the project timing.

What is the minimum quantity?

MOQ depends on the product and branding method. The catalog keeps MOQ visible so the recap stays aligned with realistic production logic.

What file should we upload for the logo or artwork?

Vector files are preferred when available, but the form also accepts common image formats. If the endpoint rejects the attachment, send the artwork by email after submitting the request.

How are lead times handled?

Lead times shown here are indicative. Final timing depends on artwork readiness, branding method, and whether the request mixes several product families.

How is merchandising different from full custom uniforms?

Merchandising starts from ready product families configured for branding. Fully custom uniforms are developed from scratch around roles, fit, operations, and long-term continuity.
