EVERYTHING IS PERSONALISED

Clear options, practical decisions

The sections below explain how personalisation is handled in real project terms, from fabric choice to branding details.

Fabrics

Fabric selection is based on shift length, climate, movement, and care requirements.

  • Examples: cotton blends, linen blends, technical easy care fabrics
  • Main criteria: comfort, durability, drape, and washing behaviour
Colors

Color direction starts from the space and the brand, then moves through operational use.

  • Examples: mill stock shades for speed, custom colors for larger programs
  • Main criteria: consistency across roles and behaviour under different lighting
Accessories and trims

Trims add identity and finish without reducing daily performance.

  • Examples: branded buttons, zips, labels, piping, contrast stitching
  • Main criteria: durability, ease of replacement, and visual coherence
Branding methods

Branding is chosen according to garment type, frequency of use, and the level of visibility needed.

  • Examples: embroidery, tonal embroidery, woven labels, screen print
  • Main criteria: longevity, legibility, and restrained placement

FAQ

Clear answers before the first call

Expanded responses to common questions from hospitality, medical, and service teams.

Do you work from a catalogue?

No. We develop each project from your operational context, brand direction, and team structure. Existing bases can be used where they genuinely improve speed and cost, but the final result remains specific to your project.

Can you support reorders after the first production?

Yes. Approved patterns, fabrics, trims, and branding methods are archived for continuity. This reduces redevelopment, improves consistency for new hires, and makes future ordering easier.

Do projects need to start with a full uniform wardrobe?

No. Some clients begin with one garment category or one role, then expand into a wider program. We define the right entry point based on urgency, budget, and operational impact.

Can you develop custom colors and branding details?

Yes. We can work from mill stock color libraries for speed or develop custom colors and trims when quantities support it. Branding methods are selected according to durability, legibility, and garment type.
